# Expense Reimbursement Policy

This article covers how to submit expenses, what receipts are required, per-diem limits, the approval chain, when you will be paid back, and how to dispute corporate card charges. All expenses are managed in **ExpenseHub** (https://expensehub.internal.example).

## Submitting an expense report

1. Sign in to ExpenseHub with SSO.
2. Click **New Report** and pick the correct type: Travel, Business Meal, Office/Equipment, Training, or Other.
3. Add each expense line with date, merchant, amount, currency, and category. Card transactions from the corporate card feed appear automatically under "Unassigned Transactions" — drag them into your report instead of typing them manually.
4. Attach receipts (photo or PDF) to each line. The mobile app's receipt capture is the easiest path: snap the receipt at purchase time and it OCRs into a draft line.
5. Select the cost center (defaults to your department; project-billed work needs the project code from your project manager).
6. Submit. The report routes automatically through the approval chain below.

Submission deadline: expenses must be submitted within **60 days** of the transaction date. Older expenses require VP approval and may be denied.

## Receipt requirements

- Itemized receipts are required for any expense of **$25 or more** (or currency equivalent).
- Credit card slips showing only a total are NOT sufficient for meals — the itemized receipt is required (alcohol must be identifiable and is reimbursable only for client entertainment with pre-approval).
- Hotel bills must be the full folio, not the booking confirmation.
- Lost receipt: complete the **Missing Receipt Affidavit** inside ExpenseHub (max 3 per calendar year; not usable for expenses over $75).
- Receipts in a foreign language are fine; add a one-line description. ExpenseHub converts currency automatically using the transaction-date rate.
- Do not submit personal card statements with unrelated transactions visible — crop to the relevant charge.

## Per-diem limits

Daily meal limits while traveling (no receipts required if you claim per-diem instead of actuals — choose one method per trip, not both):

| Location tier | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner | Daily total |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (major metros) | $20 | $25 | $50 | $95 |
| Tier 2 (all other cities) | $15 | $20 | $40 | $75 |

- Travel days count at 75% of the daily total.
- Meals provided by a conference or client reduce the per-diem by that meal's amount.
- The city-to-tier mapping is maintained in ExpenseHub > Help > Per-Diem Tables.
- Non-travel business meals (team lunches, client meals) are actuals-with-receipt only, capped at $50/person for client meals and $30/person for internal team events, with attendee names listed on the line.

## Approval chain

1. **Direct manager** — all reports.
2. **Department head** — reports over $1,000 total, or any single line over $500.
3. **Finance review** — automatic policy/compliance check on every report (receipt match, category, limits).
4. **VP approval** — reports over $5,000, out-of-policy exceptions, and late (>60 day) submissions.

Approvers have 5 business days per stage; ExpenseHub sends reminders at day 3 and escalates to the approver's manager at day 5. You can watch the current stage under Report > Status Timeline. Reports "Returned for Correction" come back to your drafts with a comment — fix and resubmit; the approval clock restarts.

## Reimbursement timelines

- Approved out-of-pocket expenses are paid with the **next payroll run** if fully approved by the payroll cutoff (the 20th of the month); otherwise the following cycle.
- Direct-deposit reimbursements typically land 3–5 business days after the "Approved – Scheduled for Payment" status appears.
- Corporate card charges are paid by the company directly — you are not reimbursed for them, but you must still submit and get them approved within 60 days, or the card may be suspended.
- Mileage is reimbursed at the standard rate published in ExpenseHub (updated each January); log start/end addresses, ExpenseHub calculates distance.

## Corporate card disputes

For unrecognized or incorrect charges on the corporate card:

1. Do NOT ignore it and do not just leave it unassigned in ExpenseHub.
2. First check with the merchant (many "unknown" charges are legitimate with an unfamiliar billing name — ExpenseHub shows the merchant's registered name under transaction details).
3. If genuinely fraudulent or wrong: in ExpenseHub, open the transaction > **Dispute Charge**. This notifies the card provider and Corporate Card Services (cardservices@internal.example).
4. Disputes must be raised within **60 days** of the statement date to be recoverable.
5. If the card itself is lost, stolen, or compromised: call the card provider's 24/7 number on the intranet Finance page immediately to freeze it, then email cardservices@internal.example for a replacement (ships in 3–5 business days).
6. Disputed amounts are excluded from your compliance totals while under investigation.

## Common rejection reasons

1. Missing itemized receipt on a line ≥ $25.
2. Claiming both per-diem and meal actuals on the same trip.
3. Wrong cost center or missing project code for billable work.
4. Alcohol without client-entertainment pre-approval.
5. Submission after the 60-day window without VP sign-off.
6. Personal expenses accidentally on the corporate card — mark them "Personal – Repay" in ExpenseHub; the amount is deducted from your next reimbursement or payroll.

## When to escalate

- Report stuck in one approval stage more than 7 business days despite reminders: use ExpenseHub > Report > **Nudge Approver**, and if still stuck email finance-ops@internal.example with the report ID.
- Reimbursement marked paid but not received after 7 business days: verify banking details in PeoplePoint first, then contact finance-ops@internal.example.
- ExpenseHub technical errors (SSO failure, upload errors, card feed missing transactions for >5 days): open a Helpdesk ticket at https://helpdesk.internal.example (category: Finance > ExpenseHub) with screenshots.
- Policy exception requests (conference over limits, team event above caps): get written pre-approval from your department head BEFORE spending, and attach it to the report.
- Suspected fraud on the corporate card: treat as urgent — freeze the card via the provider hotline first, then notify cardservices@internal.example and your manager the same day.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need pre-approval for travel?

Yes — book flights, hotels, and rail through the travel booking tile in ExpenseHub, which routes the estimate to your manager before ticketing. Self-booked travel is reimbursable only in exceptional cases and needs a written justification attached to the report.

### Can I combine personal travel with a business trip?

Yes, with manager awareness. Only the business portion is reimbursable: compare the trip cost with and without the personal extension (screenshot the comparison at booking time) and claim the lower business-only fare. Hotel and per-diem apply to business days only.

### What about home-office equipment?

Remote employees may claim up to $250 per calendar year for home-office consumables and small peripherals (category: Office/Equipment). Furniture and monitors go through the standard IT/ergonomics request process instead — do not buy them on expenses.

### Are tips reimbursable?

Yes, up to 20% on meals and reasonable amounts for baggage/housekeeping, within the per-diem or meal caps. Itemize them on the receipt line comment.

### My approver is on vacation. Is my report stuck?

Approvers can set a delegate in ExpenseHub > Settings > Delegation. If they did not, the day-5 auto-escalation moves the report to their manager, so the wait is bounded — use Nudge Approver if you are close to the payroll cutoff.

### What exchange rate is used for foreign receipts?

The corporate card feed uses the provider's settled rate; cash/out-of-pocket lines use ExpenseHub's transaction-date rate automatically. Do not convert manually — enter the original currency and amount.
