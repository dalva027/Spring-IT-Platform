# New Hire Onboarding Guide

This article walks new employees (and their managers) through everything that happens from offer acceptance to the end of the first month: accounts, equipment, badge access, mandatory training, and benefits enrollment. The HR system of record is **PeoplePoint** (https://peoplepoint.internal.example).

## Before day 1 (manager responsibilities)

Managers must complete these at least **5 business days** before the start date:

1. Confirm the hire is fully entered in PeoplePoint (HR completes this from the signed offer; verify status shows "Ready for Provisioning").
2. Submit the **New Hire IT Request** in the Helpdesk portal (https://helpdesk.internal.example, category: HR > New Hire). This single ticket drives account creation, hardware, and standard software.
3. Select the equipment bundle (see Equipment below) and note any role-specific software (e.g., IDE licenses, design tools, finance system roles).
4. Request badge access zones beyond the default (labs, server rooms, restricted floors) — these need the zone owner's approval and take longer.
5. Assign an onboarding buddy and book a 30-minute day-1 welcome call.

Late requests mean the new hire may sit without a laptop or account — the 5-day lead time is firm for shipping remote equipment.

## Day 1: account provisioning

Accounts are created automatically the night before the start date once the IT request is approved.

1. Your manager receives your username (`first-initial + last name`, e.g., `jsmith@internal.example`) and a one-time onboarding link.
2. On your laptop's first boot, sign in with the temporary credentials from the onboarding email, then immediately:
   - Set a permanent password (see the Password Reset and Account Lockout Policy for complexity rules — minimum 14 characters).
   - Enroll MFA on your phone via the guided setup.
   - Register self-service password reset at https://helpdesk.internal.example/reset/enroll (mandatory within 7 days).
3. Verify access to: email (https://mail.internal.example), the intranet (https://intranet.internal.example), PeoplePoint, and your team's chat channels.
4. Remote employees: install and test the **GlobalConnect** VPN client per the VPN Access Guide.

If any account or system is missing on day 1, the manager should reply on the original New Hire IT ticket rather than opening a new one — this keeps provisioning history in one place.

## Equipment requests

### Standard bundles

- **Standard**: 14" laptop, dock, 24" monitor, keyboard, mouse, headset.
- **Developer**: 16" laptop (32 GB RAM), dock, dual 27" monitors.
- **Mobile/Field**: lightweight laptop + LTE hotspot.

### Process

1. The bundle ships to the office or home address in PeoplePoint (verify your address before your start date!).
2. Additional peripherals after day 1: Helpdesk portal > IT > Hardware Request. Items under the standard-peripherals list (cables, headsets, mice) need no approval; monitors and specialty gear need manager approval.
3. Ergonomic equipment (chairs, sit-stand desks, split keyboards) goes through PeoplePoint > My Requests > Ergonomics and may require an assessment.
4. Report dead-on-arrival hardware within 5 days via the Helpdesk for priority replacement.

## Badge access

1. Your photo badge is prepared from the photo you upload in PeoplePoint pre-hire; collect it at the front desk / security office on day 1 (bring government ID).
2. Default access: main entrances and your assigned floor, 06:00–20:00 on business days.
3. Additional zones (labs, data rooms, 24/7 access) require a Helpdesk request approved by the zone owner.
4. Lost badge: report immediately to the front desk and open a Helpdesk ticket (category: Security > Badge) — the old badge is deactivated and a new one issued same day. A lost badge is a security event; do not delay reporting.
5. Never badge someone else in ("tailgating") — every person must badge individually.

## Mandatory trainings

All assigned in the LearnHub tile inside PeoplePoint. Deadlines are enforced; overdue training blocks system access in some cases.

| Training | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Security Awareness & Phishing | 7 days from start |
| Code of Conduct | 14 days from start |
| Data Privacy Basics | 14 days from start |
| Anti-Harassment | 30 days from start |
| Role-specific compliance (finance, engineering, etc.) | 30 days from start |

Managers can view team completion status in PeoplePoint > Team > Compliance.

## Benefits enrollment windows

- You have **30 calendar days from your start date** to enroll in health, dental, vision, and life insurance via PeoplePoint > Benefits. Missing the window means waiting for the annual open enrollment (each November, effective January 1) unless you have a qualifying life event.
- Retirement plan enrollment is available anytime; auto-enrollment at the default contribution occurs at 60 days if you take no action.
- Qualifying life events (marriage, birth, loss of other coverage) open a 30-day special window — report them in PeoplePoint within 30 days of the event.
- Questions: benefits@internal.example or the Benefits FAQ on the intranet.

## When to escalate

- No working account or laptop on day 1: manager replies on the New Hire IT ticket and calls the Service Desk (ext. 4357) citing "day-1 blocker" — these are treated as High priority.
- Benefits window closing within 5 days and PeoplePoint enrollment is erroring: email benefits@internal.example with screenshots; the window can be held open only if the error was reported before the deadline.
- Badge not ready or photo mismatch on day 1: front desk issues a temporary day badge; escalate via Helpdesk if a permanent badge is not ready within 3 business days.
- Payroll/banking details wrong before the first payday: contact hr-support@internal.example immediately — corrections after payroll cutoff (the 20th of the month) take effect the following cycle.

## First-week checklist (new hire)

1. Day 1: sign in, set permanent password, enroll MFA, register SSPR at https://helpdesk.internal.example/reset/enroll.
2. Day 1: collect badge (bring government ID); verify email, intranet, PeoplePoint, and chat access.
3. Day 1–2: install GlobalConnect VPN (remote staff) and confirm you can reach the intranet from home.
4. Day 2–3: complete profile in PeoplePoint (emergency contact, banking details before the 20th for first payroll).
5. Within 7 days: finish Security Awareness & Phishing training in LearnHub.
6. Within 14 days: Code of Conduct and Data Privacy Basics trainings.
7. Within 30 days: benefits enrollment in PeoplePoint > Benefits (hard deadline).
8. Anytime week 1: meet your onboarding buddy; bookmark https://helpdesk.internal.example for all IT/facility requests.

## Frequently asked questions

### My name is wrong / I use a different name than my legal name.

PeoplePoint supports a preferred display name (Profile > Personal > Preferred Name) which flows to email display and chat within 24 hours. Legal name changes require documentation via hr-support@internal.example.

### Can I swap my laptop bundle after starting?

Within the first 30 days, yes, with manager approval via a Helpdesk hardware request — for example moving from Standard to Developer when your role needs it. After 30 days, swaps follow the normal refresh cycle (every 3 years) unless there is a business justification.

### When is my first payday?

Payroll runs monthly with a cutoff on the 20th. If you start after the cutoff, your first payment (including the prorated period) arrives the following cycle. Details and country-specific schedules are in PeoplePoint > Pay.

### I work remotely — how do I get my equipment serviced?

Helpdesk hardware tickets for remote staff generate a prepaid shipping label; a loaner can be sent first for hard-down cases. Keep the original laptop box for the first month if you can.
